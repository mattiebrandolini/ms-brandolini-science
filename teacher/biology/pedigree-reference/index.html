  2. Mom and Dad above you, partnership line between them. Mom = circle, brown red. Dad = square, brown.

  6. Above Mom: maternal grandparents. M grandma brown, M grandpa red brown. Drop to Mom and to maternal uncle (red brown).

Things to say out loud while drawing

On dad being adopted: "My dad's biological parents — I don't know them. He was adopted. So this whole branch is dashed and unknown. That's a hard stop, not a failure. I'll still draw the shapes so we know they exist."
On grandparents you forgot: "I texted my mom mid-drawing because I wasn't sure about my maternal grandfather. She said reddish brown. That's normal — going back and checking is what scientists do."
On maternal uncle: "I thought my uncle was light brown but I'm pretty sure he has a red beard, so I'm calling him red brown. Memory is fuzzy. Updates are normal."
On step-parents: "HB mom and K dad aren't biologically related to me. They're on the chart only because they're parents of my half-siblings. They're not in my own ancestry."
On half-siblings: "Will is my dad's son with HB mom — same dad, different mom. Kip is my mom's son with K dad — same mom, different dad. Both are biologically my half-siblings. Both go on the pedigree."

The cliffhangers (next class)

1. Where does Will's blond carrier come from? His mom is blond (b/b), so she gave him a b for sure. He looks brown, so dad gave Br. Will is Br/b — brown, but carries blond. Same with Kip from K dad.
2. What's Dad's other allele? Could be Br, R, or b. We can't tell from this pedigree. Open question.
3. What about black hair? No one in my family has black hair. Our model has no place for it. Some of you have black hair in your families. What does YOUR pedigree show that mine doesn't?

The model (one-line version)

Three alleles: Br (brown), R (red), b (blond). Brown and red blend (codominant — both express). Both dominate blond. To look blond you need two blond alleles.
